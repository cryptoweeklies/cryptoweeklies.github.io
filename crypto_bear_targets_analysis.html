Methodology (TL;DR)

We estimate downside ‚Äúbottoms‚Äù for three scenarios‚ÄîBase Bear, Panic Bear, and Blood Bear‚Äîby analyzing current price relative to a composite risk score. The composite risk blends three independent risk measures: a regression deviation, a TWAP divergence, and a 20-week SMA distance, padded by a 4-year halving cycle bias.

Risk metrics

  • Regression Risk: Fit a 2nd-order polynomial trend to log(price) over the full history (with time scaled), then compute the proportional deviation (Close ‚àí Trend) / Trend. Scaled to [0,1] with a mild time ramp and halving cycle modulation.
  • TWAP Risk: TWAP is the expanding mean of price. We take the positive divergence (Close ‚àí TWAP) / Close, clip at 0, and rescale to [0,1].
  • SMA Risk: Use a 20-week (~140 trading days) SMA. Combine linear distance and proportional distance, then re-normalize.

Weighting logic

Composite Bear Risk = Reg 0.30 + TWAP 0.50 + SMA 0.20. (TWAP prioritized for finding bottoms).

Scenario definitions

  • Base Bear: Price where Composite Risk drops to 0.25. (BTC/ETH Floor: TWAP).
  • Panic Bear: Price where Composite Risk drops to 0.20. (BTC/ETH Floor: TWAP).
    *Conditional Logic: If an Alt is already <50% of TWAP and BTC/ETH are above TWAP, Panic Target = Current Price - 25%.
  • Blood Bear: Price where Composite Risk drops to 0.10. (BTC/ETH Max Drop: 20% below TWAP; Alts Max Drop: 50% below TWAP).
    *Conditional Logic: If an Alt is already <50% of TWAP and BTC/ETH are above TWAP, Blood Target = Current Price - 40%.

For each scenario we also compute a Drawdown % = Percentage drop required to hit the target.
