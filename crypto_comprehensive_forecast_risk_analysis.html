Methodology (TL;DR)

We estimate upside “peaks” for three scenarios—Base, Bull, and Moon Boy—by dividing today’s price by a composite risk score. Lower risk → higher peak. The composite risk blends three independent risk measures: a regression deviation, a TWAP divergence, and a 20-week SMA distance, padded by a 4-year halving cycle bias.

Risk metrics

  • Regression Risk: Fit a 2nd-order polynomial trend to log(price) over the full history (with time scaled), then compute the proportional deviation (Close − Trend) / Trend. We scale to [0,1], apply a mild time ramp, then modulate by a 4-year halving cycle weight (euphoria, bear, recovery, etc.) and renormalize.
  • TWAP Risk: TWAP is the expanding mean of price. We take the positive divergence (Close − TWAP) / Close, clip at 0, and rescale to [0,1]. It’s a simple read on how extended price is vs. its path-of-travel.
  • SMA Risk: Use a 20-week (~140 trading days) SMA. Combine linear distance (Close − SMA) and proportional distance (Close − SMA)/SMA (each normalized), then re-normalize the 50/50 blend.

Weighting logic

We form a composite risk as a weighted sum of the three metrics: 

Conservative mix (Bull):   Reg 0.50   TWAP 0.30   SMA 0.20

Adjusted mix (Base & Moon Boy; used when Regression Risk < 0.20):
  Increase TWAP weight by +40% and decrease Regression by −20%, then re-normalize to 1.0
  (SMA unchanged)

Scenario definitions

  • Base Peak: 0.6 × 0.95 × Price / CompositeRisk_adjusted
  • Bull Peak: 0.95 × Price / CompositeRisk_conservative
  • Moon Boy Peak: 1.5 × 0.95 × Price / CompositeRisk_adjusted

For each scenario we also compute a Reward multiple = Peak / Price.
